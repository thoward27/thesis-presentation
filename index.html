Digitizing the training requires we circumnavigate a few crucial issues.

Collaborative Design

Collaborative design involves incorporating the target population in the design process for the application to gain feedback. We applied this technique in this work as an initial investigation to gauge effectiveness of 5 distinct medium-fidelity prototypes.

Study Methodology

Study Methodology

  • Remote Synchronous Trials
  • Six participants with I/DD
  • Zoom Video Call
  • Shared virtual emulator
  • Remote control ceded where possible
  • Interview Questions + Verbal Feedback
  • We conducted these evaluations using remote synchronous trials
  • With the help of Mass Advocates Standing Strong we recruited six individuals with I/DD to participate
  • Evaluations were completed via zoom video calls
  • These sessions were bring-your-own-device, users logged in on whatever device they felt comfortable with
  • After logging in, we would share a virtual emulator running the application with them
  • and try to cede control, which worked if they were on a desktop computer
  • To evaluate the application, interview questions were asked periodically and participants were asked to vocalize as much as they could

Study Procedure

Study Procedure

  • One hour per session
  • Recorded in its entirety + researcher notes
The procedure was as follows
  • A single participant would join a session
  • We would share the emulator with them and attempt to handoff control
  • Participants would complete one of the three presented prototypes
  • After completing a lesson, they would answer a series of interview questions
  • After completing all three versions, a round of final interview questions were administered
  • These sessions typically lasted about one hour
  • And, were recorded in their entirety. Researchers also took notes during these sessions
  • Version order was counterbalanced

    Etcetera

    Emotional Reinforcement

    • Virtual pet
    • Happiness increases with training
    • Happiness decays over time
    • Maintaining happiness
    • Intrinsic motivation
    • Users were provided a virtual pet
    • Their pet becomes happier as the user completes trainings
    • Happiness decays as a function of time, so if a user does not complete a training, their pet becomes sad
    • The question here was whether this virtual pet could act as a form of emotional reinforcement to engage users and motivate them to return to the application

    Grounding Activities

    • Programmatic breaks
    • Extremely difficult content
    • Emotionally triggering
    • First-hand experience
    • This prototype proposed building in programmatic breaks throughout the application
    • this would provide users with an opportunity to ground themselves
    • the content in the application can be extremely challenging for some people
    • and, may even risk triggering certain individuals
    • Especially those with first hand experience

    Interactive Elements

    • Constant interaction
    • Increased engagement
    • Flagging abuse / not abuse
    • Exploring the impact of constant interaction
    • hopes that this would increase engagement
    • Modeled after the a&a training exercise involving flagging behavior
    • Users watch a video and mark it as abuse / not abuse
    • After the video, if they successfully identified the abusive segment, they were asked what type of abuse
    • If they failed to select the proper segment, they were offered redemption, then brought to slides if they answered incorrectly

    Quizzes

    • Presented a traditional quiz
    • Prompting users to think
    • Only quizzes
    • This prototype explored the effectiveness of presenting a quiz to users
    • the goal was to get users to reflect on their knowledge
    • Similar to most other quiz applications, presented a multiple-choice question
    • This prototype explored providing only quizzes

    Skills Activities

    • Tangential skills
    • Variety of activities
    • No Touch Zones: Sexual Abuse
    • Money Counting: Financial Abuse
    • Identifying Emotions: More General
    • This prototype asked a different question than the previous four
    • Would it be effective to just teach tangential skills?
    • It offered several different activities
    • No touch zones focused on sexual abuse by asking users to identify the no-touch zones on a human figure
    • Money counting asked users to make change up to a specified amount using US denominations
    • Identifying emotions asked users to identify emotions presented on screen
