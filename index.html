Abuse is a Problem

Abuse is a Problem

Regarding individuals with I/DD

  • 9/10 abused at least once
  • 50% experience 10+ instances
  • Often abused by known persons
  • Abuse is severely under reported
  • Valenti & Hein, sexual 1995
  • Venkatasubramanian, 2021
  • Known persons: care takers, family, friends
  • Under Reported: For example, Valenti & Hein found that only 3% of sexual abuse cases involving individuals with I/DD are ever reported
  • Faced with such a problem, what can we do?

Education as a Solution

Education as a Solution

  • Abuse prevention training is effective
  • Shown to increase confidence
  • Current solution: In person training
  • Logistics of attending in-person training
  • Frequency is important to continued understanding
  • Abuse prevention training has been shown to be effective in Krishna's work
  • Current solution: Awareness and Action in-person training events
  • In person trainings can be hard logistically to attend
  • Persons with I/DD may need help with transportation
  • Frequency has been shown to be very important in retaining information (Ayres, Buehler)

Digitizing the A&A training

Digitizing the A&A training

  • Autonomous learning
  • Refreshing knowledge vs teaching
  • Emotionally Difficult Material
  • Intrinsic Motivation
Digitizing the training requires we circumnavigate a few crucial issues.
  • Can we design something someone can use autonomously?
  • If individuals need help and the abuser is their caretaker, they won't receive it.
  • What does it mean to refresh compared to teaching?
  • Teaching means the user may have never seen the material, whilst refreshing assumes they've been exposed already
  • How can we get individuals to return to the application enough to properly maintain their knowledge?

Key Questions

  • What aspects of interactive content inspired by ideas of gamification work for the refresher application?
  • How are grounding activities built into the application received by the audience?
  • Can an application effectively refresh an individual's knowledge from the Awareness and Action training?
